Map Products to Lifecycle Stages

Pega's portfolio makes the most sense when viewed as a lifecycle map. Some capabilities reduce friction in discovery and design, some support workers during execution, and others provide runtime orchestration and model access.

  • Use Blueprint when the problem is design acceleration.

Advanced Integration

MCP Service – Extending to External AI

What is MCP?

MCP stands for Model Context Protocol. It's a bridge between Pega and external AI systems. The MCP Service allows a Pega GenAI Agent to connect with outside models or data sources to get information or delegate tasks beyond what's in the Pega app itself.

In simple terms: MCP is an interpreter that lets our Pega Agent "talk" to external AI or services. This could be another AI agent, a large language model running outside Pega, or an external knowledge database.

Why Have MCP Integration?

External systems often have valuable intelligence. Your company might have:

  • Custom AI models for fraud detection
  • Powerful cloud AI services
  • Specialized external data sources

The MCP connection broadens the capabilities of Agents by giving them access to outside data or expertise, while maintaining controlled and secure access.

Real-World Scenario: Fraud Detection

Imagine the insurer uses a third-party AI service to assess fraud likelihood. The Pega developer sets up an MCP connection to that service. Now, the GenAI Agent can automatically call this service with claim details to get a fraud risk score. The adjuster sees: "External analysis suggests a low risk of fraud for this claim" – without leaving the Pega app.
