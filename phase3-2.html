Thinking in Product Capabilities, Not Feature Names

A deep product understanding helps teams choose the right GenAI capability for the right moment in the lifecycle. It is easy to lump everything into one "AI assistant" idea, but the platform's product suite covers very different jobs: solution design, authoring acceleration, knowledge retrieval, guided work, orchestration, and model integration. When teams understand those distinctions, they can design solutions that are more targeted, governed, and easier to adopt.

The practical question is not "which Platform AI feature is most impressive?" but "which product component best reduces friction in this business process?" Some use cases need faster design-time generation. Others need real-time worker assistance or grounded answers from enterprise content. A mature architecture often uses multiple products together, each doing the job it is best suited for instead of stretching one product beyond its intended purpose.

  • Design-time tools accelerate discovery and application shaping.
  • Runtime tools support live user interactions, retrieval, and automation.
  • Integration components connect the platform to models, tools, and data services.
  • Portfolio literacy helps architects combine products intentionally instead of accidentally.
