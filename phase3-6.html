3.6

CoE Best Practices & Enterprise Governance

~2.5h

Apply Center of Excellence (CoE) patterns for enterprise GenAI governance. Implement architectural guardrails, model selection frameworks, and production deployment standards.

🏠

Always Use the Gateway

All LLM calls must flow through Platform GenAI Gateway — never bypass with direct API calls. Ensures consistent auth, logging, rate limiting, and cost attribution.

⚡

Fast vs Smart Model Strategy

Use fast/cheap models (GPT-4o-mini, Gemini Flash) for high-volume classification tasks. Reserve premium models (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5) for complex reasoning and generation.

🔒

Prompt Masking Always On

Enable PII masking for all customer-facing GenAI features. Mandatory for healthcare, financial services, and public sector deployments.

🎯

Intentional Model Selection

Document model choice rationale for each use case. Consider: accuracy, latency, cost, data residency, and vendor lock-in risk. Review quarterly.

CTC/CoE Recommendation: Establish a GenAI Practice Lead role within each product team. The Practice Lead is responsible for model governance, prompt library management, evaluation cadence, and cross-team knowledge sharing.
Topic Guide

What a GenAI Center of Excellence Actually Does

A Center of Excellence exists to turn scattered AI experiments into a managed delivery capability. It defines standards, reusable patterns, approved providers, prompt review practices, evaluation approaches, security controls, and escalation paths. Without that structure, each project invents its own rules, which leads to duplicated effort, inconsistent risk posture, and a growing number of AI solutions nobody fully owns.

The most effective CoEs balance enablement with control. They do not just approve or reject ideas; they help teams move faster by publishing templates, reference architectures, and operating guidance. In a Platform environment, the CoE should connect business architecture, workflow design, AI governance, and platform engineering so that teams can deliver quickly without bypassing enterprise policy.

  • Standards create common patterns for prompts, evaluations, approvals, and logging.
  • Enablement gives delivery teams playbooks, coaching, and reusable assets.
  • Oversight helps classify risk and define which controls are required for each use case.
  • Scale becomes possible when governance is reusable instead of bespoke.
