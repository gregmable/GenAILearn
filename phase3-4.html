Five Agent Categories

✎ Design Agents

Blueprint, Pega GenAI design-time assistants that generate application artifacts from natural language.

💬 Conversation Agents

Customer-facing chatbots and voice agents powered by GenAI. Handle intent recognition and multi-turn dialogue.

🔧 Automation Agents

Autopilot-driven case automation. Execute multi-step workflows autonomously with AI decision-making.

Topic Guide

Why Multi-Agent Systems Are Different from One Assistant

Multi-agent systems work by distributing responsibility across specialized agents instead of asking one model to reason, retrieve, plan, execute, and explain everything alone. This can improve modularity and control because each agent can be tuned for a narrower role. It also introduces orchestration complexity: someone must decide when to delegate, how to pass context, how to reconcile outputs, and when a human needs to intervene.

AgentX is valuable because it gives Pega teams a structured way to manage that orchestration inside enterprise workflows. Rather than building ad hoc chains of prompts, teams can define roles, intents, tool access, and approvals more explicitly. The design goal is not maximum autonomy at all costs. It is reliable collaboration between agents, systems, and people, with enough governance that the workflow remains understandable and auditable.

  • Specialization helps each agent focus on a narrow, well-governed task.
  • Coordination determines how tasks are handed off and how results are merged.
  • Human oversight remains essential for high-impact or ambiguous outcomes.
  • AgentX gives these patterns a runtime structure that fits business workflows.
Worked Example

AgentX Example: Claims Triage to Resolution

Picture an insurance claims journey in which a customer uploads photos, a claim narrative, and a police report. A single assistant could summarize the case, but a multi-agent design can split the work more safely. One agent extracts structured facts, another checks policy coverage, another drafts the case summary, and a human reviewer approves the settlement recommendation before it is sent.

1

Intake Agent

Collects documents and normalizes the incoming claim package.

2

Analysis Agents

Extract facts, assess coverage, and retrieve policy guidance in parallel.

3

Coordinator

Merges results into one case brief and proposed next action.

4

Human Approval

Adjuster reviews the recommendation before customer communication.

Design lesson: multi-agent systems are most useful when roles are explicit and approvals are built into the workflow, not when autonomy is expanded without boundaries.
