Topic Guide

What Makes GenAI Different

Generative AI extends beyond prediction into creation. Traditional analytical AI estimates outcomes such as churn risk, fraud likelihood, or next best action by learning patterns in labeled data. Generative AI models also learn patterns, but they use those patterns to produce new language, images, code, or summaries. That shift makes GenAI much more flexible for knowledge work, but also introduces new risks around hallucination, misuse, and explainability.

the platform's AI vision treats GenAI as a capability inside enterprise workflows, not a novelty layer on top of them. In practice, that means the model must serve the business process, respect governance controls, and work alongside deterministic logic, case management, and analytical AI. The strongest implementations use GenAI to accelerate drafting, summarization, retrieval, and assistance while preserving human accountability for meaningful decisions.

  • Analytical AI answers questions like "what will happen?" or "who should we prioritize?"
  • Generative AI answers questions like "what should we write, summarize, or explain?"
  • Enterprise value comes from pairing models with workflow, data, and policy controls.
  • the platform's approach focuses on governed, embedded AI rather than disconnected experiments.
