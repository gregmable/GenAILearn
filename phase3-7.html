Topic Guide

Readiness Means More Than a Working Demo

Production deployment is where many promising AI pilots fail because the surrounding operational requirements were ignored. A working demo proves that the model can generate an answer. It does not prove that the system can handle identity, role-based access, logging, fallback behavior, scaling, incident response, cost management, and support ownership. Deployment readiness is the discipline of making those hidden dependencies explicit before launch.

Enablement matters just as much as infrastructure. End users need to know what the assistant is for, how to interpret its answers, when to escalate, and what limitations still apply. Support teams need telemetry and runbooks. Governance teams need audit evidence. Business owners need success measures. When those capabilities are in place, AI moves from an interesting feature to a trustworthy business service.

  • Operational readiness covers scaling, resilience, monitoring, and incident handling.
  • Security readiness covers permissions, data boundaries, approvals, and audit logs.
  • User readiness covers training, adoption guidance, and clear usage expectations.
  • Business readiness covers value metrics, ownership, and long-term support funding.
Business Operations

AI FinOps and ROI for Sustainable Deployment

Production AI programs fail when value and cost are not managed together. FinOps for GenAI translates technical usage into business language: cost per task, cost per resolved case, and value per interaction. This gives stakeholders a clear operating model for deciding when to use premium reasoning models, when to route to lower-cost models, and when to require human handling. The objective is not lowest cost; it is highest reliable business impact per dollar.

FinOps Controls
  • Model tiering: route simple tasks to fast, low-cost models and reserve premium models for complex reasoning.
  • Token budgets: define soft and hard limits by use case, team, and environment.
  • Prompt efficiency: reduce repetitive context and optimize retrieval payload to control inference spend.
  • Cost anomaly alerts: detect sudden spikes by model, endpoint, or workflow action.
ROI Measurement Baseline
  • Track baseline cycle time and error rates before AI rollout to prove delta impact.
  • Measure avoided manual effort and escalation reduction per workflow.
  • Publish monthly value scorecards: adoption, quality, savings, and risk indicators.
  • Define stop/go thresholds for scaling AI features into additional business units.
