Topic Guide

Responsible AI Is an Operating Discipline

Responsible AI is not a final review gate applied after a model is already built. It is a delivery discipline that shapes data selection, prompt design, human approval, logging, access control, and model monitoring from the start. Enterprise GenAI systems can create legal, reputational, and operational risk quickly if they generate harmful content, expose sensitive information, or take actions without enough oversight.

For Pega teams, governance should align directly to workflow criticality. Low-risk drafting assistants can tolerate more automation than customer-facing decision support or agentic workflows that trigger downstream actions. The practical goal is to define where human review is required, what must be logged, which data can be shared with the model, and how failures are detected and corrected. Good governance enables scale because it gives teams a reusable operating model instead of forcing every project to invent one.

  • Fairness asks whether the system treats groups consistently and avoids biased outcomes.
  • Transparency makes it clear when AI is involved and what evidence supported the answer.
  • Accountability defines who approves, overrides, and owns the system in production.
  • Safety controls include guardrails, red teaming, content filters, and audit logs.
