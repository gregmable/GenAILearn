Topic Guide

Why RAG Matters in Enterprise AI

Retrieval-augmented generation improves answer quality by separating knowledge storage from language generation. Instead of expecting the model to memorize current enterprise content, a RAG pipeline retrieves relevant passages at runtime and inserts them into the prompt. The model then answers using that context. This gives teams a practical way to keep answers current without retraining every time a policy, product, or document changes.

The quality of a RAG system depends on more than just the model. Document chunking, metadata strategy, embedding quality, retrieval tuning, citation handling, and evaluation all influence whether users trust the output. In Pega, Knowledge Buddy and the Vector Knowledge Store handle much of the plumbing, but architects still need to decide what content belongs in the index, how access should be enforced, and what fallback behavior should happen when retrieval is weak.

  • Embeddings convert content into vectors that support semantic lookup.
  • Retrieval finds the most relevant chunks for a user's question.
  • Generation synthesizes a response from the retrieved evidence.
  • Trust increases when the answer includes sources and stays grounded in indexed content.
Worked Example

RAG Example: HR Policy Assistant

Imagine an employee asks, "How many days of parental leave do I get in the UK?" A plain LLM might answer from general internet patterns and produce something plausible but wrong. A RAG-enabled assistant instead searches the company's latest HR policy documents, retrieves the exact parental leave section, and uses that text as source context before generating the answer.

1

User Ask

Employee asks a policy question in natural language.

2

Search

Vector search finds the best-matching HR policy chunks.

3

Ground

The model receives the retrieved text plus the user question.

4

Answer

The response cites the policy and avoids inventing unsupported rules.

Design lesson: the model is still generating the wording, but the enterprise system controls the knowledge boundary. That is the practical reason RAG improves trust for policy, support, and knowledge-assistant use cases.
